    website

    I've told several people now how easy it is to make a website like this one, and I still believe it to be true. It's free, and can be easily replicated. I could teach you how to do it in half an hour. All the tech is open source.

    Paying for anything, in my experience, means giving money for the experience of being gaslit as to how necessary the tool you are paying for really is. Putting a target on your back to be marketed to, suckered into more subscriptions, taught how to interact with a black box, with skills that are not intended to be transferable.

    This kind of website, contrawise to the wooly eyes of consumer products, can easily be repurposed, recycled. Once you learn how to catch these digital fish, you can become a vegetarian. Put the pond scum on your garden.

    The only part that trips me up is I'm mirroring it both on Github and another site called Codeberg, to make it even more resistant to any kind of corporate capture or censorship.

    Mask

    [image]

    Here is the mask that I ordered a month or so again. Cattail (one of the village's many committee's, for those not steeped in our specialist nomenclature) paid for it, so it doesn't just belong to me.

    It's meant to protect your lungs from cancer-causing silica when crushing glass. Ideally, we would also have safety goggles. I'm not sure how well this gear would work with tear gas, but this function did occur to me.

    My only ask would be that if you use it, return it to and seal the plastic bag, which will extend the life and functionality of the removable pancake-filters.

    The next step in improving the safety of the glass crusher is to get the attached shop-vac unit working, or to replace it. If anyone wants to help me with this, I imagine it would be creditable hours. If not, I would compensate you personally in exchange for my own edification and peace of mind.

    Metaphors

    I've been thinking about how to repurpose, or recycle, my role as the village recyclist, to make it open source, adaptable, and modular.

    Part of my job description is education and outreach, but I wouldn't presume to charge anyone for this sort of research and writing, because I don't intent to stay on brand, or on script, or confine myself to observations flattering to the village bureaucracy, or relevant to its mission, or in line with the dessicated obscurantist style it seems to prefer.
